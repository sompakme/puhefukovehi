Marshall William Faulk (born February 26, 1973) is a former collegiate and professional football player in the United States who spent twelve seasons as a running back in the National Football League (NFL). He was a two-time consensus All-American in collegiate football for San Diego State University. He was the second overall selection in the 1994 NFL Draft by the Indianapolis Colts, and he also played professionally for the NFL’s St. Louis Rams. Only three NFL players (Marcus Allen and Tiki Barber are the others) have accumulated at least 10,000 running yards and 5,000 receiving yards; Faulk is the only one with 12,000 rushing yards and 6,000 receiving yards. In 2011, he was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame. He now works for the NFL Network as a pro football commentator for NFL Total Access, Thursday Night Football, and NFL GameDay Morning.
