Career

The first daughter of Nigeria is a qualified medical microbiologist. However, she does not practice in her field of training. Instead, she pays more attention to her humanitarian work and business ventures.

She is the founder of a non-governmental organization known as Assisting, Caring, and Empowering Charity Africa, commonly known as ACE Charity Africa. The organization assists children who have sickle cell disease. It offers free treatment for them.

ACE also actively advocates for better healthcare for all Nigerians and supports the education of children from resource-poor settings in the country. Additionally, it promotes the economic empowerment of young people and women and offers alternative education for out-of-school children.

She is also the founder of ZMB Homes. The homes offer safe and nurturing spaces to orphans in Nigeria, so they can get a new chance to achieve their dreams and purpose. In July 2018, she commissioned one home in Kaduna to accommodate and nurture 90 orphaned and vulnerable children.
