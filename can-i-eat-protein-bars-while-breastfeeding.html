Is it true that a high-protein diet is harmful to a nursing mother?

While a high-protein diet during pregnancy may have a significant impact on embryonic lethality, birth weight, and survival in the second half of life, a high-protein diet supplied to a breastfeeding mother may have a significant impact on her children during lactation.

How much protein do I need during nursing?

Nursing moms need two to three meals of protein per day, or at least 65 grammes, according to the current Dietary Guidelines for Americans. Breast-feeding mothers may achieve their protein requirements by including a nutritious source of protein in each meal.

Is it OK to consume protein shakes when pregnant?
