Early Life & Education

Tamron Hall, now at the age of 53, was born on September 16, 1970, in Luling, Texas.

She grew up in a family with a military background, as her father served in the U.S. Army for three decades. Her mother was an educator, instilling a passion for learning and knowledge in Hall.

She acquired her Bachelor of Arts in Broadcast Journalism from Temple University in 1992. After graduating, Hall moved to the Dallas-Fort Worth area to kickstart her broadcasting career.

Career

Hall’s career in journalism took off when she worked as a general assignment reporter at KBTX-TV in Bryan, Texas.

She then moved on to KTVT in Fort Worth, Texas, where she continued to build her skills and experience as a reporter.

From 1997 to 2007, Hall worked for WFLD in Chicago, Illinois, holding various positions such as general assignment reporter, consumer reporter, and host of the “Fox News in the Morning.”

During this time, she covered numerous breaking news stories and gained recognition for her reporting on Chicago politics.

In July 2007, Hall joined the national news networks MSNBC and NBC News. She served as a general reporter and fill-in anchor, gradually making a name for herself in the field.

Hall became a substitute anchor for Keith Olbermann on “Countdown with Keith Olbermann” and later co-hosted “The Big Picture” with David Shuster.

She also appeared as a substitute anchor for Natalie Morales on the “Today Show” and anchored weekend news programs.

Hall’s career flourished with the launch of her show, “NewsNation with Tamron Hall,” in 2010. The show covered various topics, including U.S. and global news, and featured high-profile interviews.

In 2013, Hall added another feather to her cap as the host of “Deadline: Crime with Tamron Hall” on Investigation Discovery.

This weekly newsmagazine series revealed various crime occurrences and highlighted the details surrounding each case.
