Gary England (born October 3, 1939) worked as the chief meteorologist at KWTV (channel 9), a CBS-affiliated television station in Oklahoma City. Using a commercial Doppler weather radar, England was the first on-air meteorologist to warn his audience of a potential tornado. He is also credited with helping to develop the First Warning map image, which is used to provide continuous weather warnings without interrupting normal programming.
