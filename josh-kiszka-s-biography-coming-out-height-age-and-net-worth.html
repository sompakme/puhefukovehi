The other members of the band are drummer Daniel Robert Wagner (Danny) and Josh's brothers, guitarist Jacob Thomas Kiszka (Jake) and bassist Samuel Francis Kiszka (Sam). Creating a band had always been Jake Kiszka's dream. Meeting Kyle Hauck in their high school band furthered that goal.

The two began to practice music in the Kiszka family garage and later recruited brothers Jake's twin brother, Josh, and his younger brother, Sam, to form a band. The band got a name after Hauck heard a relative mention Gretna Van Fleet — a Frankenmuth resident.

They even sought her authorization to modify the name from Gretna Van Fleet to Greta Van Fleet. Additionally, she supports them even though she is not passionate about music.

Hauck, the drummer, only recorded three songs with the band: Cloud Train, Highway Tune, and Standing On. He left the band in October 2013 and was replaced by Danny Wagner — a close friend of the Kiszka brothers.

About five years after Hauck's departure, Greta Van Fleet band signed with Lava Records in March 2017 and released their debut studio EP, Black Smoke Rising, a month later. Their debut single, Highway Tune, topped the Billboard US Mainstream Rock and Active Rock charts in September 2017 for four consecutive weeks.

The band's second EP, From the Fires, was released on November 10, 2017, alongside a second single, Safari Song. The EP enabled the band to win the 2019 Grammy Award for Best Rock Album.

Their debut full-length studio album, Anthem of the Peaceful Army, dropped on October 19, 2018. The album topped the Billboard Rock Albums chart in the first week after its release, and its first single, When the Curtain Falls, became the band's third number-one single on the US Billboard Mainstream Rock chart.

Anthem of the Peaceful Army also debuted atop the Billboard Hard Rock charts and reached the number one spot on the Billboard Top Album Sales charts in the first week after its release.
