How Do You Get From Philadelphia To Atlantic City

You Wanna Escape Philly for the Jersey Shore? Here's Your Guide to Getting to A.C.

Thinking of ditching the cheesesteaks for a taste of boardwalk fries and casino lights? Atlantic City beckons, my friend, and getting there from Philly is easier than squeezing into those Liberty Bell replicas (although that is an impressive feat, gotta say).

Buckle Up, Buttercup: Driving to A.C.

This is the classic Jersey Shore move. You blast Bruce Springsteen, roll down the windows, and cruise down the Atlantic City Expressway, the salty breeze whipping through your hair (or wig, no judgment). Be warned, there might be some "Shore Traffic" - those summer Fridays can get hectic. But hey, the freedom of the open road (well, mostly open) is pretty sweet.

Pros:

  • You can pack your beach essentials (read: giant inflatable flamingo) without a fight.
  • Detour for a Jersey Diner breakfast. Because, well, it's a Jersey tradition.
  • Sing along terribly to your favorite 80s hits with no one to judge (except maybe your fellow travelers).

Cons:

  • Ugh, tolls. Bring quarters or have your EZ-Pass handy.
  • Gas prices might make you cry. Just a heads up.
  • A.C. parking can be a nightmare, especially during peak season.

The Chill Option: Train Time, Baby!

For a more relaxed vibe, hop on the NJ Transit train from 30th Street Station. Sit back, people-watch, maybe crack open a good book (or that magazine you've been meaning to read for months). This is a great option if you're planning on hitting the casinos right after you arrive, because those drinks can sneak up on you!

Pros:

  • No battling traffic. You can unwind and avoid the rage of a thousand frustrated drivers.
  • Scenic views of the New Jersey countryside (hey, it has its charm!).
  • Perfect for a group trip - chit-chat, catch up, plan your A.C. adventures.

Cons:

  • Trains have schedules, you know, so some flexibility might be required.
  • Depending on where you're staying in A.C., you might need to grab a cab or rideshare from the station.

Bonus Round: The PATCO Subway

This is a local favorite for those who want to avoid the highway madness. It's a little bit of a commute (think two-ish hours), but it's a cheap and scenic option. Just be prepared to share the ride with some interesting characters - that's part of the Jersey Shore experience, right?

How to Get to A.C.: The FAQ

  • How long does it take to drive to A.C. from Philly? The drive itself is about an hour and ten minutes, but factor in traffic, especially on weekends and holidays.
  • How much does the train cost? Prices can vary depending on the day and time, but generally range from $12 to $19.
  • Is there parking at the train station in Philly? Yes, there are parking garages near 30th Street Station, but they can be pricey.
  • What's the best way to get around in A.C. once I'm there? Most of the casinos are clustered together, so walking is totally doable. There's also a jitney system (shared taxi vans) that are a cheap way to get around.
  • Can I take an Uber/Lyft to A.C.? Absolutely! Just be prepared for surge pricing, especially on weekends and during peak hours.

So there you have it, folks! Your guide to conquering the journey from Philly to A.C. Now get out there and enjoy that sweet taste of Jersey Shore fun!
