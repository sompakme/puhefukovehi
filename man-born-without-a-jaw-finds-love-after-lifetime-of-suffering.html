Man born without a jaw finds love after lifetime of suffering

Publish date: 2024-08-22

After being bullied his entire life because he was born without a jaw, Joseph Williams claims to have been rescued by love after decades of feeling "worthless."

Now he wants to remind others who are dealing with difficulties that they, too, deserve more out of life.

The 41-year-old Chicago man was born with otofacial syndrome, a rare congenital disorder caused by a mutated gene that left him unable to speak or eat with his mouth. Instead, he communicates through sign language and an eating tube.

"Dating was also hard for me because I had such low self-esteem and felt worthless, but when I started believing in myself and realized I deserved more, I ended up finding my wife," he said in a New York Post article.

Williams, a welder, had long assumed he would spend the rest of his life alone until he met his soulmate Vania, 39, in 2019.

A lifetime of struggles

Everyone wanted to shield him from the world as he grew up, telling him he "couldn't do this and that - but I didn't want my condition to hold me back and I didn't want to be limited."

Williams learned to communicate by using sign language, gestures, his phone, and writing notes, all while eating through a special tube in his stomach.

Being born without a jaw "came as a surprise" to his birth mother. He was taken to Chicago for multiple maxillofacial surgeries when he was only a few days old.

"Growing up was hard and being born like this has caused me many problems, but I have tried not to let it affect me," he said.

Today, Williams vows that his physical condition will not prevent him from living his life to the fullest with Vania, and his plans include a passion for drumming and sharing music.
