Macaulay Culkin: Home Alone actor sued by clinic over N571k medical bill

Publish date: 2024-09-22

- Macaulay is accused of not footing his bill of ($1500) N571,619 after receiving medical aid from a certain clinic

- The facility is seeking a judgment against the actor for the unpaid bills indicated to be going as far back as March 2018

- Macaulay's representatives declined to comment on the lawsuit

A medical clinic in Manhattan, New York, is suing Home Alone actor Macaulay Culkin for owing them $1500 (N571,619).

According to a Supreme Court lawsuit, the clinic is reported to have offered medical and/or hospital service or supplies at the actor's request.

Page Six reports that the hospital is seeking a judgment against the thespian for the unpaid bills, which are indicated to be going as far back as March 2018.

The facility, Manhattan Endoscopy Center, LLC, treats gastrointestinal issues, according to its website.

Macaulay's representatives declined to comment on the lawsuit.

Macaulay vs Trump

Recently, Legit.ng reported that the 40-year-old thespian showed his support for an online petition calling to remove Donald Trump's scene in the Home Alone 2 movie.

Macaulay played the role of a naughty boy called Kevin McCallister in the 1992 Christmas comedy.

The petition called for the former US president's cameo appearance to be digitally replaced by the older Macaulay.

In the movie, a young Macaulay runs into Trump at the Plaza Hotel in New York City and asks him for directions to the lobby.
